Tip No Tax Privacy Policy

This policy explains how Tip No Tax handles information when you use the iPhone app to scan receipts, confirm amounts, calculate pre-tax tips, and split checks.

Last updated: April 29, 2026 Developer: Dongjie Zhang Contact: crusade.ray@gmail.com

Privacy Overview

Tip No Tax is designed as a focused receipt utility. The app reads receipt text on the device with Apple's Vision framework, lets you manually confirm or edit amounts, and calculates tips from the pre-tax subtotal. The app does not require an account and does not intentionally upload receipt images or receipt text to a Tip No Tax server.

Receipt scanning is local Receipt OCR runs on device through Apple Vision, without a server OCR workflow.
No receipt history account The app does not ask you to sign in or sync receipt history through Tip No Tax.
Third-party SDKs disclosed The project includes Google Mobile Ads and Firebase dependencies, described below.

Information You Provide in the App

When you use Tip No Tax, you may enter or scan receipt amounts such as subtotal, tax, receipt total, tip percentage, and split count. These values are used to calculate the tip amount, final total, and per-person amount.

  • Receipt images are used for text recognition after you choose to scan a receipt.
  • Detected amounts are shown for review and can be edited before use.
  • The app's core calculator flow does not create a cloud receipt archive.
  • The app does not require your name, email address, phone number, or a user account.

Camera and Receipt OCR

Tip No Tax requests camera access so you can scan receipt totals. The camera is used only when you start scanning. Text recognition is performed on the device using Apple's Vision framework.

The app may enhance the image locally to improve OCR accuracy. The detected receipt text is parsed for likely subtotal, tax, tip, and total values so the app can offer a useful starting point for your manual confirmation.

Information We Do Not Intentionally Collect

Tip No Tax is not designed to collect or store personal receipt data on a remote server. In the core app workflow, we do not intentionally collect:

  • Your receipt images or raw OCR text on a Tip No Tax backend.
  • A history of restaurants, meals, purchases, or check totals tied to an account.
  • Payment card numbers, bank information, or payment credentials.
  • Contacts, calendars, location history, or address book data.

Third-Party Services

The Tip No Tax project includes dependencies for Google Mobile Ads and Firebase Analytics. These services may process limited technical, advertising, diagnostics, or usage information according to Google's policies and your device settings.

  • Google Mobile Ads: Used to display ads and measure ad performance. Google may use advertising identifiers, device information, and ad interaction data.
  • Firebase Analytics: May be used for app analytics, diagnostics, or aggregate usage measurement. This helps understand app stability and feature usage.
Receipt content note: Tip No Tax's receipt scanning feature is designed around local OCR. The app does not intentionally send receipt images or receipt text to Google Mobile Ads or Firebase as part of the tip calculation workflow.

Storage and Retention

Tip No Tax's calculator data is used to show current results. The app does not provide a receipt history feature or cloud sync in the current product scope. If local preferences are stored, they remain on your device unless you clear app data or uninstall the app.

Data processed by third-party services is retained according to the relevant service provider policies and your device privacy settings.

Your Choices

  • You can deny or revoke camera permission in iOS Settings.
  • You can enter receipt amounts manually instead of scanning a receipt.
  • You can limit ad tracking or manage personalized ads through iOS privacy settings.
  • You can uninstall the app to remove local app data from your device.

Children's Privacy

Tip No Tax is a general utility and is not directed to children under 13. If you believe a child has provided personal information through the app, contact us so we can review the issue.

Changes to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy when the app changes, when App Store requirements change, or when third-party services are added, removed, or configured differently. The latest version will be posted on this page with an updated date.
