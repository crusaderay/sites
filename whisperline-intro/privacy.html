Privacy Policy

Privacy Overview

Whisperline is an early-stage prototype focused on nearby encounter discovery and intentional connections. This page explains what data the current MVP handles, why it is used, and where that data lives.

At a Glance

👤

Account and Profile Data

The MVP handles authentication-provider account data plus the profile fields you intentionally enter, such as nickname, contact, and offers.

📡

Nearby Encounter Metadata

Bluetooth-based nearby discovery creates lightweight encounter metadata that helps the app detect repeat real-world meetings.

🔐

Restricted Sharing

Connection records and feed visibility are limited to intentional 1st connections rather than being public by default.

Information We Collect

  • Account information returned by your authentication provider
  • Profile fields you enter, such as nickname, contact, and offers
  • Connection records you intentionally create
  • Nearby encounter metadata generated by BLE discovery in the app

How We Use Information

  • To sign you in and maintain your account session
  • To let you edit your profile and share it with approved connections
  • To aggregate repeat encounters into candidate suggestions
  • To debug and improve the MVP during prototype testing

Storage and Permissions

☁️

Supabase-Backed Data

Account, profile, and connection data are stored in Supabase for core app functionality in the current MVP.

📱

On-Device Encounter Data

Encounter sessions, candidate aggregation, and local settings are stored on-device in the current prototype.

📍

BLE and Device Permissions

The app requests Bluetooth-related permissions to discover nearby devices and exchange lightweight profile-card information. Optional location hints may be used depending on platform permissions and future feature rollout.
