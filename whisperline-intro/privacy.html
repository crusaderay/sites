Whisperline is an early-stage prototype focused on nearby encounter discovery and intentional connections. This page explains what data the MVP handles and why.

Information we collect

  • Account information returned by your authentication provider.
  • Profile fields you enter, such as nickname, contact, and offers.
  • Connection records you intentionally create.
  • Nearby encounter metadata generated by BLE discovery in the app.

How we use information

  • To sign you in and maintain your account session.
  • To let you edit your profile and share it with approved connections.
  • To aggregate repeat encounters into candidate suggestions.
  • To debug and improve the MVP during prototype testing.

Storage and access

  • Account, profile, and connection data are stored in Supabase for app functionality.
  • Encounter sessions, candidate aggregation, and local settings are stored on-device in the current MVP.
  • Feed visibility is restricted to deliberate 1st connections rather than the public.

BLE and device permissions

  • The app requests Bluetooth-related permissions to discover nearby devices and exchange lightweight profile-card information.
  • Optional location hints may be used by the app to label encounters, depending on platform permissions and future feature rollout.
